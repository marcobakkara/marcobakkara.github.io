Wisdom from Great Minds

The only true wisdom is in knowing you know nothing.
Socrates
Greek Philosopher (469-399 BC)
Excellence is an art won by training and habituation.
Aristotle
Greek Philosopher (384-322 BC)
It does not matter how slowly you go as long as you do not stop.
Confucius
Chinese Philosopher (551-479 BC)
The mind is everything. What you think you become.
Buddha
Spiritual Teacher (563-483 BC)
Wise men speak because they have something to say; fools because they have to say something.
Plato
Greek Philosopher (428-348 BC)
A journey of a thousand miles begins with a single step.
Lao Zi
Chinese Philosopher (6th Century BC)
You have power over your mind - not outside events. Realize this, and you will find strength.
Marcus Aurelius
Roman Emperor & Philosopher (121-180 AD)
Let yourself be silently drawn by the strange pull of what you really love. It will not lead you astray.
Rumi
Persian Poet & Mystic (1207-1273 AD)
